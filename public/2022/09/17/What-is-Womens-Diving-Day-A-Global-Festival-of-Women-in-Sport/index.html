What is Womens Diving Day A Global Festival of Women in Sport

What is Womens Diving Day A Global Festival of Women in Sport?

Diving day is a festival of Women in sport, It is to show the world that Women can do Sport and they are not just for watching.

The event was founded in 2013 by Sarah Stevenson. Sarah Stevenson is a British former world champion and Olympian in diving. She is also the founder of Divers For Diversity, a not-for-profit organisation which encourages greater diversity within diving, and sport overall.

In 2013, Stevenson decided to create an event to celebrate women in sport, and Diving Day was born. The inaugural event took place on 8th March 2014 – International Women’s Day.

Each year, Diving Day gets bigger and better! Events have taken place all over the world, from the UK and USA, to Mexico, Canada and beyond! There have been talks, workshops, dive sessions, film screenings and more – something for everyone who wants to get involved!

So what does Diving Day 2019 have in store? This year’s theme is ‘Women Leading Change’. We want to celebrate all the amazing women out there who are making a difference in their communities and beyond. We want to hear your stories of how you are leading change – whether it’s through sport, activism or simply being yourself.

If you would like to get involved in Diving Day 2019 (either as an individual or organisation), please get in touch! You can find us on Facebook (www.facebook.com/divingday) or Twitter (@divingday). Alternatively, email info@divingday.org.

Now in its fifth year, Diving Day continues to go from strength to strength! We hope you will join us in celebrating women in sport this year!

When is Womens Diving Day A Global Festival of Women in Sport?

The inaugural Women’s Diving Day is a global festival of women in sport taking place on 1st December 2019. The day is coordinated by the Women’s International Dive Federation (WIDF), and celebrates the outstanding achievements of women in diving, and across all levels of sport.

The day is open to everyone, with events taking place around the world to get more girls and women involved in diving, and sport more broadly. This could be anything from giving someone their first ever try at diving, to holding an exhibition match or tournament, to running a sports workshop or seminar.

Why Women’s Diving Day?

Despite being one of the most popular Olympic sports, with huge spectator interest and some of the most dedicated fans in the world, diving has long been seen as a male-dominated domain. In recent years there have been great strides made to change this, but there is still much work to do.

One way to help address this imbalance is by celebrating the successes of women in diving, and inspiring more girls and young women to take up the sport. Women’s Diving Day does just that, providing a focal point for all things female-diving related, and showcasing the amazing talent that exists within the sport.

Who can get involved?

Women’s Diving Day is open to anyone who wants to get involved. Whether you’re a dive enthusiast or complete beginner, whether you compete at an elite level or just like to dabble recreationally; if you want to celebrate women in diving then Women’s Diving Day is for you!

How can I get involved?

There are lots of ways you can get involved in Women’s Diving Day: hold an event yourself, sign up for or organise an event hosted by someone else, or simply show your support online using #WomensDivingDay. You can find out more about how to get involved on the WIDF website (widf.org).

Where is Womens Diving Day A Global Festival of Women in Sport?

First celebrated in 2014, Women’s Diving Day is a global festival of women in sport. This day celebrates the amazing accomplishments of women everywhere in the sport of diving, and works to raise awareness of the barriers that still exist for women in sport.

Despite being one of the oldest sports in the world, women’s participation in diving has only recently begun to be taken seriously. There are still many challenges and obstacles that face female divers, ranging from a lack of resources and opportunity to unequal pay and discrimination.

Female divers have worked hard to break down these barriers, and on Women’s Diving Day we celebrate their achievements and continue to work towards equality for all. This day is also an opportunity to encourage more girls and women to get involved in diving, as well as other sports, and show them that anything is possible with hard work and determination.

So what can you do to help celebrate Women’s Diving Day? Here are a few ideas:

  1. Share your favorite female diver on social media using the hashtag #WomensDivingDay.

  5. Follow Women’s Diving Day on social media for updates on events and activities happening around the world! The official Women’s Diving Day Facebook page can be found here: www.facebook.com/womensdivingday/.

Why is Womens Diving Day A Global Festival of Women in Sport?

On the 5th of March each year, Women’s Diving Day is observed globally to celebrate the achievements of women in sport, and more specifically, in diving. This day was founded by Anita DeFrantz, an Olympic gold medalist in rowing and advocate for gender equity in sport. The aim of this day is to create greater visibility for women in sport and break down barriers that have been preventing them from achieving their full potential.

One of the main reasons why Women’s Diving Day is so important is because it helps to shine a spotlight on the many injustices that women face when trying to participate in sport. For example, female athletes often receive less funding and media coverage than their male counterparts, despite performing just as well or even better. Additionally, there are many cases of discrimination and sexual harassment towards female athletes, which can prevent them from reaching their full potential.

By observing Women’s Diving Day each year, we can help to raise awareness of these issues and work towards creating a more equitable sporting landscape for women. Not only does this benefit women directly, but it also has a positive impact on society as a whole. When women are given equal opportunities to participate in sport, they can achieve amazing things both on and off the field. This is something that we should all be striving for!
